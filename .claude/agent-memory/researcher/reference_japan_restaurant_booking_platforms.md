## Booking Platform Usage by Tier

| Platform | Market position | Typical users |
|---------|----------------|--------------|
| **TableCheck** | Domestic standard, SaaS | Hiramatsu, NELU, Hamadaya, mid-to-high tier |
| **一休 (Ikyu)** | Premium domestic | Narisawa, wide Michelin-star coverage |
| **OMAKASE (omakase.in)** | High-end, international-facing | Narisawa, Fujiya1935, Florilège |
| **Pocket Concierge** | English-speaking inbound market | Narisawa, Kikunoi |
| **TABLE ALL** | Exclusive access specialist | Kikunoi, Ishikawa |
| **Phone only** | Deliberate exclusivity signal | Kichisen, Tofuya Ukai |
| **Pre-payment (credit card/PayPal)** | Growing trend to filter no-shows | Ajikitcho |

## Key Patterns
- No restaurant builds their own booking system — all delegate to platforms
- Most 2-star+ restaurants list 2-3 booking options to serve domestic + inbound tourists
- Phone-only = luxury signal (used by most exclusive establishments)
- Pre-payment is growing as a no-show reduction mechanism
- Reservation availability messaging on the website ("Opens daily at 10am for 2 months ahead") creates urgency and sets expectations

## For MOTTO Demo Builds
When showing reservation flow in demos: link to TableCheck or OMAKASE as the external platform. Never build a custom form. The CTA button should say "予約する" (JP) / "Reserve" (EN) and open the external platform.
